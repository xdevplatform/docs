Articles let you publish long-form, richly formatted content natively on X — think of them as full blog posts that live right in the platform. The Articles API gives you programmatic control over the complete creation and publication flow.

## The two-step flow

Publishing an article always follows two steps:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a draft — POST /2/article/draft">
    Send your article's `title` and `content_state` (the structured body content). Receive an `article_id` in return.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish the draft — POST /2/articles/{article_id}/publish">
    Submit the `article_id`. X publishes the article and automatically creates a Post linking to it. You receive the new `post_id`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Authentication & requirements

<Note>
**Requirements**

- **Authentication:** OAuth 2.0 User Context — your app must be authorized by the user whose account will author the article
- **Scopes:** Read and Write permissions
- **Subscription:** The authenticated user must have an active **X Premium (Blue Verified) subscription**. Non-premium users receive a `403 Forbidden` response.
</Note>

---

## Article content model

Article bodies are described using a structured `content_state` object — a block-based document model similar to a rich-text editor. Each block represents one paragraph, heading, list item, or embedded element. Inline formatting (bold, italic, links) is applied as ranges on top of the block's plain text.

See the [Writing Guide](/x-api/articles/writing-guide) for a full explanation plus a copy-paste builder utility that takes care of offset math for you.
